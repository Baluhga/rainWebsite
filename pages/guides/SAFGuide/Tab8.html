Calculations for Skills

Feel free to entirely skip this section if you are not interested in in-depth calculations of skills/phials and general effectiveness of builds.  This section will be a bit technical by default so if you don’t understand much here, @ me in discord (mel177371) and I will happily answer all your questions, concerns and suggestions regarding this section.

The calculations I did in this section were done using the damage calc on Doriels website , which works absolute wonders for calculating every tick of damage you do to a monster in frontier. I highly suggest learning how to use it as it is one of the key worktools that allowed me to explore different meta options which were not considered in the past. The walkthrough on this website is a good way to get introduced to the tools on this site, try it out if you want.

Another important thing to note:   I did most of these calculations by myself using models that I found appropriate while using sources for the various parameters. These calculations are not to be interpreted as the undoubted truth! I also make mistakes so if you think something may not be applicable as I have calculated it, please dm me and let’s discuss it. I want to be as mathematically accurate as possible but it’s hard to be completely unbiased when having to work with example cases.

Important information ahead:

  • Every monster has a certain  true HP (THP)  (often 30k but not always, exceptions are for example Musou Zinogre and Upper Shiten Disu), true HP divided by it’s defense rate gives you the effective HP (EHP)
  • The calculations show damage numbers that will be done to the monsters THP , taking into account it’s defense rate  (so even though e.g. Narga and Elz have 30k THP, the damage numbers on a hitzone with the same numerical value will be different because the defense rate is different)
  • The reference monster, its hitzone and applied buffs/skills/weapon stats/sigils will be mentioned at the start of the given section or referenced
  • Alot of these calculations assume certain scenarios that I will mention beforehand, or reference in any existing Swaxe run
  • Often the term Hitzone value (or in short HZV)  will be used in this section to refer to the hitzone that a monster has on a particular body part (i.e. the multiplier on the dealt physical/elemental damage on this monsters part)

Importance of Perfect Defense

Perfect Defense (lg) is a caravan skill that you unlock by leveling your caravan gem to a certain point and it does the following:

If you input a guard/parry within 4 frames before  a monster's attack hitbox connects, you will activate perfect defense (which we will call PD) which cancels knockback from the parry/guard and reflects damage back to the monster. The base mv of PD is 72 which is very high and gets raised to 92 if you run Reflect+3 and Reflect Up+1 in your set (which you usually do in matchups where you can PD). You can only pd if you have enough stamina for a roll (without including Taijutsu), which means if you use CS+SW you are not able to use PD. We will purely focus on the 2 primary cases where you are always able to PD (Zino and Elz). If you run Reflect+3 and Reflect Up+1, your parries (outside of the PD window) will also reflect back damage with a mv of 68, so PDing a move instead for 92 mv meaning 24 mv more on every pd compared to normal reflects, which I probably won’t need to tell you is much better. PDing every attack instead of normal reflecting it means you have a total damage increase of ~24% for every reflect proc, which is something that you should absolutely get used to, especially when doing legion where you will most likely not be able to run CS.

Z Sigil Math class

By now you should have noticed how often I dropped the word Z Sigil in my builds. These are sigils in the Zenith category which you can roll for using Zenith monster parts. They can be directly used like items but without having to sheathe. They have both a duration and a cooldown. The most important Z sigils and their stats will be listed here:

Wait Time : Up to 17, reduces base cooldown (120 seconds) by the listed number

Duration : Up to 12, increases the base duration (15 seconds) by the listed number

(for Z Atk) Attack : Up to 15, gives 20*Attack+30 raw during uptime (up to 330)

(for Z Ele) Element : Up to 15, gives 0.1*Element+1.3 multiplier during uptime (up to 2.8)

Ele phials vs. Power phials

Yes, in some matchups (namely Boga, Zeru and Deviljhos) elemental phials are not only able to compete with power phials, they are straight up better!  There are a few reasons for that:
First of all, aside from the different phial effects both Flash and Shine have the same raw, but Shine has more element ! Combine this with the 1.3x Ele Boost of elemental phials, the 1.2x Ele Boost from elemental attack fruits and Furious, the 1.1x Ele Boost from all ele drug, the 2x Ele Boost from transcend (which you can use in many of these matchups!) and the 2.8x Ele Boost during sigil uptime of a perfect Z ele sigil, and you get mind blowingly large elemental numbers up to 18k  for a lvl 100 Shine evo with combined uptimes and appropriate set/sigils.
The Second reason is that Swaxe gets 15 additional elemental HZV from dissolver, which is the highest tied with weapons like MS etc. This is not the case for all weapons (like Tonfas which only get 5) so this is another important point to note.

What is the strength of Obscurity Up ?

Alot of old Swaxe builds that were optimized for Musou hunts (especially the CS ones) used only  Stylish for sharpness recovery. It recovers 5 units of sharpness every time you iframe an attack which is more than enough to keep up the sharpness over an entire hunt without any issue. Problem is: without its Zenith Up skill, Stylish doesn’t provide any damage, while Obscurity Up does.   Before getting into what Obscurity Up does, let’s see what Obscurity itself does. Obscurity gives you raw damage which stacks up every time you parry. It stacks up to 225 raw for Swaxe after 10 parries total (+30 raw per parry on first 5 parries, +15 raw per parry on the last 5). You lose all of the raw once you get knocked down by a monster . What Obscurity Up does is it reduces the total parry requirement for the max raw buff from 10 to 6 (+50 raw per parry on first 3 parries, +25 per parry on the last 3) and PD procs will now act like 3 parries towards the build up. After reaching the maximum amount of parries, each parry recovers 4 units of sharpness (10 if it is a PD). The sharpness recovery after a PD is visualized by a blue aura appearing every time you PD an attack. From this alone it is not hard to see why Obscurity Up is so much better than Stylish: you get a way faster Obscurity build up while maintaining sharpness nearly the same way. Of course in some cases you can potentially drop to purple using CS and Obscurity Up if you are fighting for a long time with only few parries (generally only in speedruns) but besides that you should generally have no sharpness problem.

Another advantage of gaining Obscurity quickly with Obscurity Up is that you can start stacking Stylish Assault earlier, which means you get a faster damage spike at the start of the run. Since you can drop sharpness near the end of CS runs if you’re not fast enough, you can alternatively also run Stylish Up builds.

How much damage does Ceaseless Up really do?

One of Frontiers endgame skills that you see on alot of sets (especially alot of CS sets that are listed on here) is Ceaseless Up . What Ceaseless Up does is it adds a 3rd stage to the Ceaseless skill which on top of what Ceaseless already provides gives +0.05 additional crit damage multiplier and 10% affinity (you can completely ignore the affinity and concentrate on the crit damage since with the scaling of crit con 10% affinity provides around 2 true raw). To give context to what these numbers mean here a following itemization of crit multipliers in this game:

  • Solid Determination: +0.25 crit damage
  • Ceaseless Stage 2: +0.15 crit damage
  • Starving Wolf+2: +0.1 crit damage
  • Ceaseless Stage 3 (Ceaseless Up): +0.05 crit damage
  • Total crit multiplier without Ceaseless Up: 1.75
  • Total crit multiplier with Ceaseless Up: 1.8

The crit multiplier is a multiplier on motion value , not raw damage! If the motion value ends up having a decimal, it will always get rounded down to the lower value (meaning even in a case of for example 50.9 MV the actual MV would get rounded down to 50). This is an important point to make because this (besides its overall strength) will weaken this skill even further when compared to damage skills that offer a direct raw damage increase.

It is very important to note that the Upslash and S Evasion Upslash (and it’s charged counterpart), one of Swaxes highest DPS tools, gets rounded down from 93.6 to 93 (154.8 to 154 for the charged one).  The effectiveness of Ceaseless Up depends on the decimal of the MV at both stage 2 and stage 3. If we take the average damage increase and compare it to increasing weapon raw by an arbitrary amount, we would roughly get a similar increase when going from 770 to 830 (meaning 60 extra true raw, in reality it’s less because of the upslash damage loss) , which can at least give us a rough estimate of its strength for a non-CS set on one of its usual matchups. This is of course not definite and things could  change depending on what skills you run on a set. Now it is important to note that this assumes 100% uptime of the skill, but to activate it in the first place we need a combination of 17 slashes/reflects/stylish up procs  (from stage 2 to stage 3) and lasts for 12 seconds before falling back to the second stage after not landing more slashes/reflects/Stylish Up procs. For Swaxe specifically the time needed to activate stage 3 of Ceaseless isn’t that long (especially in reflect matchups).

This means that Upslash and Vertical Slash get an MV increase of 3 instead of Upslash having 2 like before and the charged Upslash gets an MV increase of 5 instead of 4. In this case Ceaseless Up provides a higher percentage damage increase overall compared to the SW+2 case.

Another important point to touch on is that Ceaseless Up also lowers the required amount of hits to reach stage 1 and stage 2 respectively. Why I haven’t greatly mentioned this is because for switch axe specifically this doesn’t have a large effect since switch axe is a fairly fast hitting weapon. It’s still important to talk about since it gains us some points of true raw but not as many as for example for slow hitting weapons like gs, hammer, ms etc.

How good Stylish Up really is

Here is where it gets interesting. This discussion will mainly include Elz as a reference monster, but what is said here also works for Zino . What is important to note about Elzelion is a very interesting mechanic: for every projectile move that Elzelion does (Icicle/Fireball throw), if you PD and fade slash immediately afterwards, you will proc Stylish/Stylish Assault on the fade slash.  This is very important and I’ll show you why in the following comparison: Reflect+3 and Reflect Up raise the MV of a PD from 72 to 92, while Stylish Up provides a motion value of 30 when you iframe an attack, meaning that in the case of Elz’s projectiles, you would have a combined mv of 72+30=102. Now since you also want to account for keeping your stylish assault up on top of that, you would get damage in where you purely iframe attacks (which has to happen within a 20s window) and lose a bit of damage on regular pd’s. For this fight it can be said that both Stylish+Up and Reflect+Up provide good value for Elz specifically  but the latter doesn’t punish you as hard for not PDing constantly.

An interesting talking point would also be running Stylish+Stylish up for cs sets instead of Obscurity Up+Ceaseless Up (or Ice Age). The net damage gain from this skill increases the more you iframe attacks (meaning stylish up has high value against monsters with a high attack frequency like narga or zino) but it does less damage the slower the monster is (e.g. boga/zeru).

Another interesting talking point is pairing stylish up with consumption slayer instead of Ceaseless Up and sharpen with a perfect whetstone mid-fight. This works very well for Zino/Elz during the phase transition and after depleting the crystals you can keep up the sharpness very well with stylish up. The advantage of consumption slayer instead of Ceaseless up is that it also increases PD and stylish up damage. In a run with optimal RNG this can give a non-negligible boost of damage  (~40-50 more raw if we compare with effective raw+more pd/stylish up damage).

A very interesting combination of skills is also bringing both Stylish+Up and Reflect+Up instead of Ceaseless Up/Obscurity Up and Ice Age. This works wonders against both of the example cases (Elz and Zino) because you get the consistent 20 motion value PD increase of Reflect Up and can pair this with the 30 motion value from Stylish Up on alot of Elzes attacks. Not having a perfect PD rate will also net you more damage with this build than the normal stylish up build since it still provides 68 motion value on a normal parry instead of none. It seems this could be the most consistent and highest damage combination for Elz.

How good is ice age

Ice age is generally run on nearly every set apart from very few specific cases but it’s important to discuss its effectiveness during a hunt. What ice age does is upon hitting the monster you get an icy aura which gives your teammates stamina recovery up and sharp sword, but this is the boring part. The aura deals damage over time to the monster and has 3 different stages:

Stage 1 : (0.042 x True Raw x DefRate) DPS, 11-18s duration, 7s when downgraded to

Stage 2 : (0.098 x True Raw x DefRate) DPS, 14-25s duration, 10s when downgraded to

Stage 3 : (0.14 x True Raw x DefRate) DPS, 6-9s duration

The more you hit the monster, the higher the stage you reach until you reach stage 3 which gets refreshed upon every hit on the monster. Now what becomes pretty clear is that ice age does less damage the lower the defense rate of the monster is (as can be seen in the formula), however something important to note is that ice age ignores hitzones aswell . This makes this skill a good candidate for consistent damage on a monster with bad hitzones (e.g. Zino) and a rather replaceable skill for a monster with alot of EHP and very good hitzones (e.g. Boga). Another sad part about the skill is also that it only scales with weapon/sigil/sr true raw and not with additional gained raw throughout the fight. For a fast Elz run of ~8 minutes of fighting (30 seconds of preparation) the total damage of ice age dealt throughout this fight would be 480x4=1920 which is 6.4% of his HP as “free damage”. You reach a similar number vs Zino over half the fight time (5.6%) just by the difference of their defense rate and since Boga for example is a monster with tons of EHP which still dies comparatively fast (~5:10 of fighting), it only deals around 3% to his THP over the course of the fight, the conclusions to draw from this should be obvious.

When it comes to dropping the skill, the noteworthy mentions would be cs+reflect sets that also run stylish assault (for elz e.g.) but these are very niche cases because most endgame sets for Swaxe already contain alot of the best damage skills in the game. This means that you want to usually include ice age as a free damage component in your sets.

When you should run Elemental Attack Up

Elemental Attack Up is an interesting skill in that it provides a very simple buff to element (additional 1.1 multiplier) but has a snowball effect when combined with every other elemental multiplier in the game and ends up being a very significant damage increase with every uptime. It is essential for every Ele Phial matchup and essentially replaces Ice Age because of its overall higher damage increase. In contrast to this you never really want to take it outside of Ele Phial matchups because for Power Phials (especially against monsters with a low defense rate) the percentage damage increase for element is very much negligible.

Phial Consumption/Tech Cost Reduction Info

Extreme Style Lightsword Mode has a static cap of 300 “Phial Units”. At the start of a quest or when you revive after fainting you will always have 150 Phial Units. Parries restore 75 units or 150 during active feature. Edge Marshal reduces Phial Unit costs to 0.75x or a 25% reduction to all attacks that spend Phials. These numbers are based off of the “Gauge Consumption” numbers listed on the SAF Motion Value Table on Wycademy . Shoutout to platinumdragoonx for parrying pokara in the tundra and burning phials at camp to figure this out.

The method used to arrive at these numbers assumes that the values from Wycademy are true. The first test performed was without Tech. After getting full phials (2 parries w/ no active) I stood in base camp and performed Vertical Slashes/Upwards Slashes (both 10 phial usage according to Wycademy) until the phial meter was empty. This required 30 swings in total (30x10 = 300 hence the 300 maximum phial units and 150 starting phial units). The second test was the exact same but with Tech and required 38 swings from full to reach empty. 38 divided by 30 = 1.267 which I round down to 25% for convenience sake.
