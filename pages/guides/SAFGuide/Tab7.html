Speedrunning

In contrast to the Musou/Shiten section, this section will focus on actual min-maxed builds and tactics that are used to reach the speedrun times/compete with current records. This section will most likely be updated over time if something is found out to be more efficient.

The structuring of the builds section will be different to the one in the Musou/Shiten Monsters section but will of course include the builds that are (so far) mathematically the best sets to use against musous, while trading a lot of comfort or being in general harder to play because they rely on specific things that need to be trained over a lot of tries (consistent pd’s, risky openings/punishes etc.). Included in here will also only be monsters that are actually part of the Rain Speedrunner quest section (which are also generally the quests that people speedrun the most in MHFZ), not the ones that are not included.

What will not be discussed here are speedruns using prayer gems! The best option for prayers is usually to switch out sharpness recovery for consumption slayer, Kairi is by far the most knowledgeable when it comes to stuff like that.

Presented here will be builds for speedrunning. These will always include the version that is considered the highest DPS but  still consistent enough at a monster so that it wouldn’t require an unrealistic RNG to finish the run (CS elz would be an example of a build that would require that, hence it’s not included). The reason why these skills were chosen the way they were will be briefly explained in the matchup details and have a detailed reason in the In Depth Info section.

Matchup details

In here I will try to put my thoughts on different matchups into words (and sometimes example clips) to show you how to avoid the hardest moves in the matchup and what to generally do. Of course reading these notes alone will not make you the next Swaxe legend but they may give you hints on how to approach certain matchups.
Please contact me if you want more to be included here. Most of these notes were written out of my own experience and this section is more experimental than anything, so feel free to leave feedback.

Boga

Intro: After entering the area, run to his right arm, parry the explosion. After morphing, you should position under his arm and start slashing to build up PB. Fadeslash once for Stylish Assault and then parry to stack Obscurity early.

Matchup Details: Get used to having high slash uptime, and animation cancel your downslash with a fadeslash (on attacks like his drag this mechanic is very important). Don’t use charged slashes aside from his run or unless you know you are gonna miss a downslash because he raises his arm (hard to do that preemptively).

Example Runs: Example Run 1  (ele phials) , Example Run 2   (ele phials), Example Run 3  (power phials, outdated build), Example Run 4  (Poison Swaxe, watch only first half)

Zino

Intro: Run up to the spot where he starts dashing at you, parry the dash, fade back immediately (safer if you PD) and parry again. Then morph, run to the edge of the thunder nuke hitbox and use your Z Atk Sigil.

Matchup Details: Get used to PDing all of his attacks. The higher your PD rate, the higher your damage output. Position yourself so you can consistently slash the head (fade back or left after his slams, fade back on his dome to charge slash his head, fade back on his slams). Every time he turns, it’s a free opportunity to get a PD or Stylish Up proc since the turning is a damage hitbox.

Example Runs: Example Run 1  (with CS only) , Example Run 2  (Non CS but with Z-Attack), Example Run 3  (without Z-Attack)

Guan

Intro: Go into the area, wait for him to land. Then parry the landing, instantly morph and run up to him to start slashing his forearms.

Matchup Details: Play around his head partbreak threshold. Every time it is reached he will topple which opens up the window for a discharge. If his head is not reachable, attack his forearms since they have a very good slash hitzone aswell. Try to keep up base Stylish Assault and parry whenever you get the opportunity, since the run is extremely short you will not get max buffs at the end of the run.

Example Runs: Example Run 1  (with Z-Attack), Example Run 2  (without Z-Attack)

Unknown

Intro: Eat a Shiri Fruit, run up to him and align yourself so that you morph and hit his head with the lightsword transformation. If the fadeslash hits the head, you can immediately run away for a guaranteed phase transition. If you don’t hit the head, you need one more slash to reach the HP threshold.

Matchup Details: The entire first phase of Unknown is scripted, after you think you reached a damage threshold try to stay to the side or at a distance to her. After every first attack, she will cycle through attacks but if she reaches the HP threshold and has to move to do an attack (turn or run), she will do a phase transition instead. In her last phase, slash as aggressively as you can onto her legs (for a potential trip). Keep up your Stylish Assault and your gauge. Her priority hitzones are legs, head and tail, but keeping up slash damage is the most important so even if you don’t hit her best hitzones you can still get a fairly good time.

Example Runs: Example Run 1  (with script) , Example Run 2 , Example Run 3

Zeru

Intro: Wait for Zeru to land, stand right outside of the damage hitbox, parry the tremor and morph. Do 4 attacks on Zeru before fadeslashing the wind pressure, use your Z Ele Sigil while he’s in the air and parry the lasers.

Matchup Details: Slash very aggressively on his wings. His head is also a decent hitzone, his tail is by far the best hitzone but having high slash uptime on the wings is easier. Get used to sniping his wings on both the air explosion and his double stomp into divebomb attack. Try to keep track of your Obscurity stacks early on and stack it to full as fast as possible, and then only parry if you need gauge. Learn to abuse his phase transition for damage (stand inside and iframe the scream).

Example Runs: Example Run 1  (ele phials), Example Run 2  (ele phials), Example Run 3  (power phials), Example Run 4  (power phials+prayers)

Narga

Intro:

Most effecient intro:   After bombing into the area, run up a bit and buff up while he is preparing to nuke. Parry his intro, morph into lightsword mode, fadeslash->upswing and parry the quick tailslap.

Safe intro : After entering the area run up to him, parry the dash and run from the vacuum. Then morph, run to his head and upslash downslash fadeslash and lastly parry the tail spins and the dash.

Matchup Details: Learn how to position yourself for certain parries. For his slash attack after his dash, it is most consistent to stand to the opposite side of his slashing arm. For every attack that involves his tail, try to stay away from the tail tip since parrying it is basically impossible. Everywhere else is fine. For his rain, wait for his head to lower after the scream and spam the parry input as fast as possible.

As for the general gameplan in the run, try to slash his head and preferably have PB on it. While abusing partbreaks for topples is also good, hitting his head consistently provided faster pace in most attempts.

Example Runs:   Example Run 1 , Example Run 2 , Example Run 3  (with prayers but beautifully played)

Jhos

Intro: Parry the scream and morph, use Z Ele Sigil, stand at the wall and hope for the best.

Matchup Details: Try to slash through both of them if possible. Be aware of their attacks and parry as often as you can (they like to layer attacks on top of each other so this is not easy). Having hitless periods in the run will give you a lot of pace but staying hitless for the entirety is insanely hard. When one of them enrages (stomps into explosion), try to stay close to him and parry the scream unless the broken ground appears below you (run away instead). Random balls are your best friend.

Example Runs: Example Run 1  (with CS), Example Run 2  (without CS)

Disu

Intro: Eat a Shiri Fruit, the next action depends on his size. If he’s medium-large, parry the ice (or the shattering after he lands) and instantly morph, if he’s small-baby run up to him in axe mode after he landed, parry the bite and then morph.

Matchup Details: The most optimal strategy is praying for the smallest size and hitting the wings consistently but since it’s frustrating to roll that size by just grinding him, his tail is usually the next best hitzone to hit. You can hit the wings on surprisingly many attacks (his lasers, flying attacks, tornados etc.) so look out for good opportunities to slash them. Buff up during phase transition. Phase 2 and 3 will become much harder than phase 1 since the speed of his attacks increase alot. For the phase transition to phase 3, it’s best to look at the first example run to see how to damage him while he’s transitioning (there is alot of damage hitboxes during that period)

Example Runs:   Example Run 1 , Example Run 2 , Example Run 3

Durem

Intro:  Run up a bit to the right, PD the scream, sidestep and PD the incoming thunderballs (the hitbox is very bad, positioning needs to be precise). Morph into lightsword mode, run to his tail and roll the scream.

Matchup Details: Always stay behind him no matter what. Get used to sniping his tail in uncomfortable positions and try to charge slash if you feel like your normal slashes would miss the tail otherwise. His pawslam is by far the hardest move to deal with, avoid it with either a parry->fadeslash or just a fadeslash into a deadzone. You should always try and position yourself in a way where you are able to immediately slash the tail after the attack (on his tail spin, fadeslashing to the back is much better than to the left for that reason). Stylish Assault uptime in this fight is not guaranteed so the longer you have it the faster your run is going to be. Try and judge the timing for his 50%/10% threshold, usually you are behind him but if he Z screams at you if you are in front of him it’s a death sentence.

Example Runs:   Example Run 1  (full Obscurity opening) , Example Run 2  (half Obscurity opening), Example Run 3  (non CS)

Elz

Intro: When you’re in the area, walk up to the edge of where the ice+fire is going to spawn, PD that hitbox (normal parry is also okay if you’re not running Obscurity Up) and then roll into the hitbox to get Stylish Assault. Run to his tail, roll out of your extreme run and wait for him to tail slam for the parry into morph.

Matchup Details: This matchup is by far the hardest to play aggressive in because this monster has very little downtime between attacks. You have to play around your hitlag and get used to slashing him a certain way while idled so he cannot surprise you with an attack. One of the most frustrating parts of this matchup is his tail hitbox, whenever you feel that you are too close to his tail on a spin try to fade away from it or parry it (or accept death if both of those are not an option). His rush move (dash move, also spawns fire+ice aoe in phase 2) is one of the most frustrating moves to deal with. Try to get used to its PD timing since PD into roll is often more safe than just trying to roll out of your hitlag.

Playing at his forearm and at his tail are two completely different tiers of difficulty (his arm is much easier to slash). On his tail, his fast tail drag in phase 2 will spawn fire/ice aoe depending on which direction he drags it into, so having fast fingers is necessary to play at his tail (the attack is very fast and can surprise you alot).

Get used to slashing very aggressively during his non element phase (after he lost his fire element) since that is the biggest damage window in the entire fight. Abuse the fact that he is really slow and does few attacks during that time window.

Example Runs:   Example Run 1  (Reflect+Stylish Up), Example Run 2  (Stylish Up only), Example Run 3  (Reflect Up only), Example Run 4  (Reflect Up only), Example Run 5  (Tower Swaxe)

Legion

Playing aggressively in this quest is a must and you should be able to get a good time if you know all the matchups even if you die. Make sure to get combine mats for teleporters and go back to base after every slay (after zino) for power crystals.
